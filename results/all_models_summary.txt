==========================================================================================
  ANALYSIS: OVERFITTING vs. UNDERFITTING
==========================================================================================
The objective was to tune models to an 85-92% accuracy range while minimizing False 
Negatives. Looking at the "Gap" column (Train Accuracy - Test Accuracy), EVERY model has 
an Overfitting Gap of less than 0.1% (< 0.001). This confirms that NONE of the models 
are currently overfitting. In fact, due to our heavy constraints, most are UNDERFITTING:

1. Logistic Regression (80.86%): Underfitting. Linear models inherently suffer from high 
   bias when faced with complex, non-linear boundaries across 35 features and 36 classes.
   Adding class_weight='balanced' strongly prioritized rare attack classes, costing 
   overall accuracy and pulling it under 85%.

2. Naive Bayes (70.96%): Underfitting. The model makes a strong assumption that all 
   network features are independent. In reality, network traffic features are highly 
   correlated, causing the probabilistic baseline to hit a hard mathematical ceiling.

3. Random Forest (84.31%): Slight underfitting. We aggressively constrained the trees 
   (max_depth=5, min_samples_leaf=20) to pull accuracy down from ~98.8%. It generalized
   perfectly (gap +0.0006) but lacks the depth capacity to easily reach higher accuracy.

4. XGBoost (97.89%): Strong fit. Even with max_depth=3 and high regularization (gamma=5), 
   the sequential nature of boosting still captured complex patterns, keeping accuracy 
   very high. It generalized almost perfectly.

5. LightGBM (41.21%): Severe underfitting. The combination of min_child_samples=100 
   and class_weight='balanced' on a dataset with a 5810x class imbalance ratio forced 
   the leaf-wise growth to prematurely stop. It lacks the freedom to partition the 
   majority classes effectively under these constraints.
